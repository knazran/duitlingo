Stocks 101

Hensem, you probably have heard about stocks/shares before - but what it actually is?

Owning stocks is equivalent to owning part of the business.

Imagine this: Ali owns a restaurant, but he wants to open two more branches.

So Abu and Ahmad provide him RM200,000 each to fund the expansion, and Ali gave them 30% of the business shares, or ownership, each in return.

Stock trading 101

The shares listed in this page, are just the same as those that Ali gave to Abu and Ahmad, but in much smaller amount & proportion.

All shareholders, big or small, would have the right to vote for the Board of Directors.

Can I trade the shares of any companies in Malaysia?

No, only share of companies that are publicly listed are tradable. Currently, there are around 920 companies listed on Bursa.

But why is the share price going up and down?

It is simply due to supply and demand, just like any other items.

If there are a lot of demand in the ownership of a stock and a limited amount of supply, people are willing to pay a higher price to buy it, pushing up the price.

In the contrary, if many people don‚Äôt think this business is good anymore and wanna sell off their ownership, then supply will be high and they will be willing to sell it at a lower and lower price.

Saham 101

Hensem, mungkin anda pernah terdengar atau terbaca mengenai saham sebelum ini - tetapi apa itu saham?

Saham itu merupakan satu unit permilikan sebuah syarikat atau bisnes.

Cuba bayangkan: Ali memilik sebuah restoran, tetapi beliau mahu membuka dua buah cawangan lagi.

Untuk membiayai kos pembukaan cawangan tersebut, Ali memberi 30% sher and permilkan bisnes restoran beliau kepada rakannya, Abu dan Ahmad. Abu dan Ahmad membeli sher tersebut pada harga RM200,000.
